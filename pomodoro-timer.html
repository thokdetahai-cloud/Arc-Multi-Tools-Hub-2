About the Pomodoro Timer

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. Work is broken into 25-minute focused intervals (called "pomodoros"), separated by 5-minute short breaks. After four sessions, take a longer 15–30 minute break.

Studies show this technique reduces the impact of interruptions, improves mental agility, and helps combat procrastination. This free timer handles all three phases automatically and tracks your completed sessions.
