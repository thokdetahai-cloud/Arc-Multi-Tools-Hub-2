About Leap Year Checker

A leap year has 366 days instead of 365, with February 29 added. The rules: a year is a leap year if divisible by 4, except century years must be divisible by 400. So 2000 was a leap year, but 1900 was not. This correction keeps the calendar aligned with Earth's orbit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Earth takes approximately 365.25 days to orbit the Sun. Without leap years, our calendar would drift by about 6 hours each year — causing seasons to shift over centuries.

Leap years occur every 4 years: 2024, 2028, 2032... unless it's a century not divisible by 400 (like 2100, which is not a leap year).
